VEX Robotics

While in high school I participated in my school's robotics program. Each year VEX came up with a game to be played. My job was to design & build a robot to compete in VEX's game. I worked with a team of 3 other students. We built the robot from scratch using metal pieces, nuts, bolts, motors, & a central system (brain) that you plugged in all the motors to. So the game itself is usually a 2 v 2 match. It is a timed 2 minute game. The first 15 seconds is the autonomous period where the robot moves strictly based off of code with no help from the 'driver'(human). My team and I developed the autonomous period code together. After the 15 seconds is up, the remaining minute & 45 seconds is driver control. This is where we can use a remote control to control the robot. We also coded what the buttons do when you press them on the controller. In 2017 I won a State Championship with my robot and team. We went on to compete at the World Championship where we competed with 600 other teams from around the world. There were 6 divisions with 100 teams being in each division. We ended up getting eliminated in the division finals. The following year in 2018, I made it to state again. This time I came up short in the semi-finals.
